Quality Inspection Services

What is Quality Inspection?

Quality Inspection, often referred to as Quality Control (QC), is a systematic process designed to ensure that manufactured products or delivered services consistently adhere to predefined quality standards and meet customer or client specifications. It serves as a critical checkpoint to verify safety, functionality, reliability, and overall compliance before goods reach the market.

As product quality has become a decisive competitive factor in the global marketplace, an increasing number of importers and buyers—especially international brands—now mandate formal quality inspection for their orders sourced from China. Implementing structured QC helps safeguard brand reputation, reduce returns, and ensure customer satisfaction.

To maintain consistent quality and minimize defect rates during mass production, many clients opt for During-Production Inspection (DPI). This proactive approach allows for early identification and correction of issues while production is still underway, preventing costly surprises and delays just before shipment. DPI is widely regarded as one of the most effective strategies to control quality at source.

Our Quality Inspection Procedure

To conduct objective, consistent, and efficient quality inspections, INSPECTION-CHINA has developed a clearly defined operational procedure that standardizes the workflow and actions of every inspector. Our process ensures transparency, accuracy, and reliability at each stage.

Prior to the inspection, we share detailed inspection standards and checklists with the supplier. This ensures they are fully aware of all critical quality requirements, enabling them to align production processes accordingly and reduce potential defects early on. By fostering clear communication and expectation-setting from the outset, we help suppliers improve their internal quality management and support smoother, more compliant production runs.

Types of Quality Inspection Services

INSPECTION-CHINA offers a comprehensive range of quality inspection services tailored to meet diverse client needs at every stage of production. We provide flexible and scalable solutions—from pre-production checks to final random inspections—ensuring quality control is seamlessly integrated into your supply chain.

Our team can promptly organize on-site quality control interventions based on your specific requirements and instructions. Whether you need an initial production check, a during-production inspection, or a pre-shipment verification, we execute with efficiency and precision.

Within 24 hours after each inspection, you will receive a detailed report complete with clear photographs or video evidence, highlighting both conformities and any non-conformities found. Should any critical issues arise during the inspection, our qualified inspector will contact you immediately to discuss findings and recommend corrective actions—ensuring you stay informed and in control throughout the process.

Pre-Production Inspection (PPI)

Pre-Production Inspection (PPI) serves as a crucial verification step to ensure that the supplier fully complies with all specifications and requirements before mass production begins. This inspection is conducted during the production preparation phase, once materials and initial setups are ready. Suppliers may proceed with full-scale manufacturing only after a satisfactory PPI result is confirmed, helping to prevent issues before they escalate.

During-Production Inspection (DPI)

During-Production Inspection (DPI) provides ongoing, objective monitoring to track production progress in real time and verify that all requirements and instructions are being accurately followed by the supplier. Typically carried out when 20–25% of production is complete, this phase allows for early detection of deviations, enabling timely corrections and ensuring consistent quality control throughout the entire manufacturing cycle.

Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI)

Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI) serves as the final, comprehensive quality check performed on fully finished and packaged goods prior to shipment. Generally conducted on 90–100% of the ready products according to client specifications, this critical inspection verifies strict conformity to all technical requirements and overall shipment readiness. Upon receiving the detailed inspection report, the client can confidently make an informed and timely decision regarding shipment approval or initiate any necessary corrective actions.

Container Loading Supervision (CLS)

Container Loading Supervision (CLS) offers end-to-end oversight of the loading process, from container arrival to sealing. Throughout this service, every step is meticulously documented, including cargo placement and container condition, to prevent discrepancies and ensure full compliance with shipping requirements. This thorough record helps avoid unexpected issues upon goods receipt and enhances shipment transparency.

Audit Services

What Is a Factory Audit?

A factory audit is a systematic and objective assessment carried out by a professional auditor to evaluate whether a supplier or vendor meets the criteria to be considered a qualified and reliable business partner. It is also designed to uncover any potential risks that could emerge during the course of future collaboration, such as quality issues, production delays, or non-compliance with legal and ethical standards. The scope of a factory audit extends well beyond a simple review of equipment and production capacity. It includes a thorough examination of how the factory organizes its production processes, ensures consistent product and service quality, and manages its workforce, including aspects such as working conditions, health and safety practices, and labor rights.

For most companies seeking to establish a partnership with a supplier they have little or no prior experience with, conducting a factory audit before making a final decision has become an increasingly common and necessary step in the sourcing process. This is particularly true for multinational corporations and international brands, for whom a comprehensive factory audit—often including an ethical or social compliance component—is now considered a prerequisite to signing any formal contract. Such audits help ensure that suppliers align with the buyer's values and operational standards from the outset.

On one hand, a factory audit helps clients build confidence in their collaboration with a supplier by providing an objective assessment of the supplier's capabilities and reliability. On the other hand, this diagnostic process enables clients to gain a clear understanding of the supplier's strengths and weaknesses, and more importantly, to identify and mitigate significant potential risks before they result in financial loss or reputational damage.

During the audit, the auditor carefully verifies all critical points outlined in the audit checklist, as well as any additional concerns or special requirements raised by the client. These may include production processes, quality control systems, raw material sourcing, maintenance practices, and compliance with health, safety, and environmental standards. Following the site visit, a detailed and objective audit report is prepared based on the evidence and observations gathered. Our audit report includes not only a completed questionnaire accompanied by photographic evidence but also videos captured during key operations and professional observations and comments from the auditor. This comprehensive documentation ensures full transparency, supports informed decision-making, and serves as a valuable reference for continuous improvement and future collaboration.

Types of Audit Services

In addition to inspection services, INSPECTION-CHINA offers a comprehensive range of audit services tailored to meet the diverse needs of our clients. All audits are conducted by our team of professional quality and management experts, who bring extensive industry experience and in-depth technical knowledge to every assessment.

Our audit services are designed to help clients evaluate supplier capabilities, identify potential risks, and ensure compliance with relevant standards and requirements. Depending on the specific objectives and scope, we provide the following types of audits:

Simple Factory Audit (SFA)

The simple factory audit is a streamlined verification process that assesses a supplier's basic structure and qualifications prior to order placement. This type of audit is typically conducted for smaller facilities or those involved in less technically complex products and services. It focuses on essential operational aspects to ensure the supplier meets the fundamental requirements for a reliable business partnership. The approach is efficient and direct, providing a clear snapshot of a supplier's capability to handle basic production needs. For buyers, this serves as a cost-effective tool for initial supplier screening and risk identification before committing to larger volumes.

Advanced Factory Audit (AFA)

The advanced factory audit is a comprehensive assessment based on the ISO9001 management system, offering a professional and detailed evaluation. This audit is typically applied to medium or large-scale suppliers and those offering technical products or complex services. It thoroughly examines quality management processes, operational consistency, and overall organizational effectiveness. By benchmarking against international standards, the audit provides deep insights into a supplier's long-term reliability and capacity for continuous improvement. As a result, it is an essential step for clients seeking to establish robust and scalable supply chain partnerships.

Supplier Social Audit (SSA)

The supplier social audit evaluates a factory's compliance with international standards such as SA8000, ISO14001, ISO18001, and BSCI frameworks. This audit accurately reflects the factory's practices regarding worker rights, employee welfare, and overall working conditions. It also scrutinizes critical areas including occupational health, workplace security, environmental impact, and ethical management. By identifying strengths and areas for improvement, it helps ensure the supply chain aligns with global social responsibility norms. For international brands, this audit is indispensable for upholding corporate values and meeting stringent regulatory requirements.

Supplier Improvement Actions (SIA)

We also provide ongoing management improvement follow-up services to help factories rectify non-conformities identified during audits. Our experts work on-site to develop practical corrective action plans and implement sustainable solutions tailored to each supplier's needs. The frequency and scope of these follow-up engagements are flexibly determined based on client requirements and the complexity of issues uncovered. This proactive service ensures continuous progress and helps maintain elevated standards over the long term. By supporting suppliers in their development, we contribute to building more resilient, transparent, and trustworthy global supply chains.
